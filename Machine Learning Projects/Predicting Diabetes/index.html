Executive Summary

This project focuses on predictive modeling utilizing a clinical diabetes dataset. A significant emphasis was placed on the foundational data integrity stages, specifically evaluating how different missing data imputation strategies and outlier treatments fundamentally altered downstream model accuracy.

Methodology & Model Performance

The workflow involved benchmarking traditional architectures against robust ensemble methods. To address severe class imbalance within the clinical features, targeted adjustments and hyperparameter tuning were applied to both Random Forest and XGBoost classifiers.

The final analysis evaluates the tradeoffs between model sensitivity (recalling true positive diabetic instances) and overall precision to provide a business-ready clinical decision framework.
